How this works

Every 5 minutes, this page pulls the current data for each matchup from ESPN, looks at each team's roster and the games they have left in the week, and runs 10,000 simulations of how the rest of the matchup might play out. Each team's Win Probability is just the fraction of those simulations they won.

What goes into a simulation

  • The matchup totals you can see right now (R, HR, K, ERA, etc.)
  • Each rostered player's ESPN rest-of-season projection, which already factors in how they've been performing this year.
  • How many games each MLB team has left in the matchup week.
  • Probable pitchers for each remaining game — so a stud SP with one start this week counts very differently from a stud SP with two starts.

Rate stats (OPS, ERA, WHIP) are always rebuilt from their underlying counters — they're never averaged across players, which is the way most quick estimates get rate stats wrong.

What it doesn't account for

The model is decent but not perfect. A few things it deliberately leaves out, sorted roughly by how much they matter:

  • Roster moves you make later this week. If you drop someone and stream a hitter Friday, the model only sees that the next time rosters refresh (every ~4 hours). Your Win Probability will lag accordingly.
  • Lineup changes — same thing. Benching a slumping bat or activating someone off the IL only flows through on the next roster refresh.
  • Pitchers being knocked out early vs throwing a complete game. The simulator uses each starter's average production per start, so a blow-up start that ruins your ERA is within the distribution but not weighted as heavily as it probably should be.
  • Teammate correlation — Judge and Stanton both having a big game is correlated in real life. The simulator treats each player's stats as independent, which slightly over-spreads the outcome distribution.
  • Reliever usage detail. Closers, setup men, and middle relievers are all treated with the same rough "appears in ~60% of team games" assumption. A closer pitching in a blowout contributes the same as a closer in a save spot.
  • Off-days, platoon splits, weather. Hitters are assumed to play in every one of their team's remaining games.
  • Probable-pitcher matching is by name. If MLB and ESPN spell a name differently, that SP may not get credited with their probable start. Rare but it happens.

Refresh cadence

  • Live matchup totals + current-week simulation rerun: every 5 minutes.
  • Rosters + projections + future-week sims: every 4 hours.
  • MLB schedule + probable pitchers (full remaining season): once a day.
